The beginning of life as we now know it...
Nikki and I had our first child yesterday (July 30, 2006). He was behind schedule and ahead of schedule at the same time. Nikki's due date was July 23rd, but since that had come and passed, the doctor scheduled an induction appointment. That was all set to begin Sunday July, 30 at 4:00pm. Saturday, after our last night out before becoming parents, the fun began.We had dinner and went out for ice cream. By 10pm Nikki was remarking how much the baby liked ice cream and was moving around like crazy. We went to bed around midnight, still not believing that this could be anything more than nerves. Promptly at 1 am. Nikki woke up with definite, strong contractions. By 1:45am we were on our way to the hospital with contractions 2 1/2 minutes apart. She was examined and had zero dilation at 3:00am. We were told to go and walk around the hospital for an hour on the off chance that something may happen. We returned to the registration area at 4:00am and she was magically dilated to 3 cm. We were on our way.

The hospital admitted Nikki right away and moved her to a birthing room. Her OBGYN was called to come in and the epidural was promptly administered. We both attempted to get a little rest, but the baby's heart rate became erratic. Different nurses and two pediatricians came and went, making sure everything was all right. The contractions continued but were hidden under the soft epidural blanket. Nikki's dilation continued, but the baby’s downward progress did not. After 2 hours of serious pushing, it was decided that a c-section would be needed. More to come later.
